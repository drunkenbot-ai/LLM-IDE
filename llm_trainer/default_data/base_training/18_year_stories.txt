The students build a small weather station on their roof. They measure rainfall, wind, and temperature. They share the data with the agriculture department. Farmers use it to plan their sowing.

The girl sees a man cutting a tree in her street. She asks him to stop. She explains the shade and fruit it provides. The man agrees and prunes only the dead branches. The tree stands for many more years.

The young man is a cobbler. He repairs shoes at a very low rate for school children. He says they need good shoes to walk to school. The children remember his kindness and bring their parents' shoes too.

The group organizes a "repair café" once a month. People bring broken clocks, fans, and toys. Volunteers fix them together. The items get a second life. The waste sent to landfills reduces.

The girl is a dancer. She performs a piece about water conservation at the village fair. The villagers see the dance and understand the message. They start fixing their leaky taps.

The boy starts a community radio channel from his room. He broadcasts local news, farming tips, and folk music. The whole village listens on small transistors. He gives a voice to the voiceless.
