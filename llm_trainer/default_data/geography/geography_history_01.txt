Earth has seven continents.
Asia is the largest continent.
Africa is also very large.
Australia is both a country and a continent.

Earth has five main oceans.
The Pacific Ocean is the largest ocean.
The Indian Ocean touches India.
The Atlantic Ocean lies between the Americas and Europe.

A map shows places.
A compass shows direction.
North is one direction.
South is the opposite direction.
East and west are also directions.

India is in Asia.
New Delhi is the capital of India.
The Himalayas are in northern India.
The Ganga is an important river.

Japan is an island country.
Tokyo is the capital of Japan.
Many people in Japan speak Japanese.
Japan has many earthquakes because it lies near plate boundaries.

Egypt is in Africa.
Cairo is the capital of Egypt.
The Nile River flows through Egypt.
Ancient Egyptians built pyramids.

Greece is in Europe.
Athens is the capital of Greece.
Ancient Greeks studied math, art, and government.
Many modern words come from Greek.

A civilization is a large organized society.
Civilizations have cities.
They have jobs, rules, tools, and trade.
They often develop writing.

The Indus Valley civilization grew near rivers.
River water helped farming.
Farms helped cities grow.
Trade connected people.

An invention is a new useful thing.
The wheel helped people move heavy loads.
Writing helped people store ideas.
The printing press helped books spread faster.

A timeline shows events in order.
First, a seed is planted.
Next, the plant grows.
Later, it makes flowers.
Order helps history make sense.

Explorers travel to learn about places.
Scientists observe and test ideas.
Leaders make decisions for groups.
Good leaders listen and plan.

Culture includes language, food, music, clothing, and traditions.
Different places have different cultures.
Respect helps people learn from one another.

Climate is the usual weather of a place.
A desert is often dry.
A rainforest is often wet.
Mountains can be cold.

Population means the number of people in a place.
A city has many people close together.
A village has fewer people.
Both cities and villages need water, food, and work.
